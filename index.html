-Pushpa

The Rise is a 2021 Indian Telugu-language action drama film written and directed by Sukumar. It is the first part of a two-part series, with the second part, Pushpa: The Rule, anticipated to continue the story. The film stars Allu Arjun in the titular role of Pushpa Raj, alongside an ensemble cast that includes Rashmika Mandanna, Fahadh Faasil, and others.

Cast & Characters

Allu Arjun as Pushpa Raj: The film's protagonist, a sharp and ambitious man who climbs up the ranks of the red sandalwood smuggling operation. Rashmika Mandanna as Srivalli: Pushpa’s love interest and a woman from his village, who plays a significant emotional role in the narrative. Fahadh Faasil as SP Bhanwar Singh Shekhawat: A menacing police officer, who is introduced at the end of Pushpa: The Rise and is expected to play a crucial role in the sequel. Jagapathi Babu as Reddy, the antagonist involved in the smuggling business. Sunil as Mangalam Srinu, one of Pushpa's allies.

-Baahubali

Directed by S. S. Rajamouli, these two epic fantasy films became a global phenomenon. The story, set in the fictional kingdom of Mahishmati, is known for its grand scale, stunning visuals, and intense drama.

Cast & Characters

Cast: Prabhas as Amarendra Baahubali / Mahendra Baahubali: The brave and heroic protagonist. Rana Daggubati as Bhallaladeva: The main antagonist, known for his physical prowess and treacherous nature. Anushka Shetty as Devasena: The strong and noble queen, who plays a crucial role in the story. Tamannaah Bhatia as Avanthika: A warrior who aids Shivudu in his mission to rescue Devasena. Sathyaraj as Kattappa: The loyal and honorable warrior who serves the royal family and plays a key role in the narrative. Nasser as Bijjaladeva: Bhallaladeva’s father, who schemes behind the scenes to ensure his son ascends to the throne.

-RRR

"RRR" (2022) is an epic action-drama film directed by S. S. Rajamouli, who also helmed the Baahubali franchise. Like Baahubali, RRR became a massive cultural phenomenon and one of the biggest blockbusters in Indian cinema history. The film is set in the 1920s during British colonial rule and is a fictionalized account of the lives of two legendary freedom fighters, Alluri Sitarama Raju and Komaram Bheem.

Cast & Characters

Ram Charan as Alluri Sitarama Raju: A passionate freedom fighter with a complex emotional journey. Charan’s performance as Raju is intense, blending strength and vulnerability. N. T. Rama Rao Jr. (Jr. NTR) as Komaram Bheem: The tribal hero fighting to protect his people and their traditions. Jr. NTR's portrayal of Bheem is powerful, full of raw energy, and emotional depth. Alia Bhatt as Sita: Raju’s love interest and a pivotal character who plays a key role in the emotional arc of the film. Ajay Devgn as Venkata Raju: A supporting role, portraying a significant character with ties to Raju's past. Ray Stevenson as Governor Scott Buxton: The cruel British governor who embodies the oppression faced by the Indian people. Olivia Morris as Catherine Buxton: The governor’s wife, who plays an antagonist role.

-Arjun Reddy

"Arjun Reddy" (2017) is a bold and unconventional Telugu-language film directed by Sandeep Reddy Vanga. It became a massive hit, not only for its raw and unfiltered storytelling but also for its unique approach to handling complex emotions, relationships, and mental health issues. The film was widely discussed for its controversial themes, portrayal of intense emotions, and unapologetic characters.

Cast & Characters

The cast of "Arjun Reddy" is led by Vijay Deverakonda as the fiery and self-destructive protagonist, Arjun Reddy, whose emotional turmoil drives the plot. Shalini Pandey plays Preeti, Arjun's love interest, whose separation from him becomes the film's central conflict. Rahul Ramakrishna portrays Shyam, Arjun's loyal friend who offers comic relief and emotional support. Sanjay Swaroop and Kanchana play Arjun's parents, adding emotional depth to his troubled family dynamics. Supporting roles are filled by Getup Srinu, Pradeep Rawat, and others, contributing to the film's varied emotional tones.
